## 1. Court Order on Hold Policies — PM 602-0192, PM 602-0194, PA 2025-26 vacated

**Source:** USCIS Newsroom Alert — https://www.uscis.gov/newsroom/alerts/court-order-on-hold-policies
**Published:** USCIS alert posted 2026-06-12 (court order issued 2026-06-05; final judgment entered 2026-06-11)
**Effective date:** Immediately upon entry of final judgment, 2026-06-11
**Document #:** Dorcas International Institute of Rhode Island, et al. v. USCIS, et al., 26-cv-00132-JJM-PAS (D.R.I.)

### What changed
The U.S. District Court for the District of Rhode Island vacated three USCIS policy instruments agency-wide: PM 602-0192 (Hold and Review of Pending Asylum/Benefit Applications from High-Risk Countries, originally 19 countries), PM 602-0194 (Additional High-Risk Countries expansion, eff. 2026-01-01), and PA 2025-26. USCIS states it disagrees with the order but will follow it pending possible further judicial review. The vacated memos must be treated as if they are not in effect. The practical result: adjudicative holds placed on asylum applications and most benefit requests from listed countries are lifted.

### What was known before
PM-602-0192 (issued 2025-12-02) directed officers to place an adjudicative hold on pending asylum applications and most benefit requests filed by nationals of 19 designated "high-risk" countries: Afghanistan, Burma, Chad, Republic of Congo, Equatorial Guinea, Eritrea, Haiti, Iran, Libya, Somalia, Sudan, Yemen, Burundi, Cuba, Laos, Sierra Leone, Togo, Turkmenistan, Venezuela. PM-602-0194 (eff. 2026-01-01) expanded the list. Ukraine and Russia were NOT on the original 19-country list. Turkmenistan WAS on the list and is relevant to a sub-segment of Russian-speaking users. The full PM-602-0194 expanded list has not been verified against this monitor's records — needs follow-up.

### Affected Messenginfo services
- Translation service — I-589 (asylum) instructions page: any prior copy referencing processing holds for nationals of listed countries is now stale.
- FAQ — sections discussing case freezes / "high-risk country" processing delays.
- User-facing copy mentioning Turkmenistan-origin Russian-speaking applicants (sub-segment).
- No direct Ukraine TPS / Ukraine I-765 / Ukraine I-821 impact — Ukraine was not on the lists.

### Recommended action
- Verify PM-602-0194 expanded list before drafting copy (specifically check whether Belarus, Tajikistan, Uzbekistan, or any other Russian-speaking country was added — these would be the only segments of our user base directly affected besides Turkmenistan).
- Update FAQ / explanatory pages to remove or rephrase any language stating that applications from "high-risk" / vetted countries are on indefinite hold.
- Add a single dated note: "По решению федерального суда от 11.06.2026 приостановка рассмотрения дел из ряда стран отменена; USCIS возобновляет рассмотрение." (informational only — no legal advice).
- Monitor for USCIS appeal or stay motion — the vacatur is in effect now but may be challenged.

### Risk level
**MEDIUM** — directly affects workflow guidance for a sub-segment of Russian-speaking users (Turkmenistan and any additional countries on PM-602-0194). Does NOT affect the core Ukraine TPS / U4U pipeline. Update within 1 week; do not delay verification of the PM-602-0194 country list.

## 2. Clarification of Discretionary Employment Authorization for Certain Aliens (Proposed Rule)

**Source:** Federal Register — https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/06/05/2026-11285/clarification-of-discretionary-employment-authorization-for-certain-aliens
**Published:** 2026-06-05 (one day outside the strict 7-day window; retained because comment period is open and policy implications for parole-based EAD users are direct)
**Effective date:** Not specified — proposed rule; comments due **2026-08-04**
**Document #:** 2026-11285

### What changed
DHS proposes to limit and clarify eligibility for discretionary EAD for three categories: (a) aliens paroled into the U.S. for urgent humanitarian reasons or significant public benefit, (b) aliens granted deferred action, and (c) aliens under final orders of removal who are released on orders of supervision. The proposal would deny favorable discretion to applicants who admit to, have been arrested for, or have been convicted of certain criminal acts unless significant countervailing public interests apply. This is a proposed rule — not yet effective. Public comment open through 2026-08-04.

### What was known before
Parole-based EAD (category c)(11)) has historically been adjudicated under a permissive discretionary standard. Uniting for Ukraine (U4U) parolees apply for EAD under c(11). Re-parole applicants and humanitarian parolees more broadly fall within the proposal's scope.

### Affected Messenginfo services
- Translation service — I-765 instructions page (especially c(11) parole category).
- FAQ — Uniting for Ukraine / re-parole / EAD application sections.
- User-facing copy that describes EAD as "routinely approved" for U4U parolees — that framing becomes risky if the final rule narrows discretion.
- TPS pipeline (I-821 + I-765 c(19)) is NOT in scope — TPS-based EAD is statutory, not discretionary parole-based.

### Recommended action
- No immediate user-facing copy change — this is a proposed rule, not effective.
- Add an internal watch entry: track final rule publication date and any change to c(11) eligibility for U4U parolees.
- Review FAQ for any unconditional statements about EAD approval for parolees; soften to "обычно одобряется при отсутствии судимостей" with neutral phrasing.
- Consider preparing a draft public comment if Messenginfo's user base is materially affected — this would need to be a separate decision, not a regulatory monitor output.
- Re-check 7-day window each Friday/Saturday through August 4 for related notices, corrections, or extensions of comment period.

### Risk level
**MEDIUM** — proposed, not final; but if finalized as drafted, directly affects EAD outcomes for a large share of U4U parolee users. Track aggressively; act on user-facing copy only after final rule publishes.
